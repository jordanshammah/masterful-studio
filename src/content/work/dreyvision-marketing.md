Page visits rose by half. The click-through rate on the queries we targeted climbed 45%, meaning more of the people who saw the listing actually clicked it. And the site's average ranking position improved by 80 places off its starting point, moving target pages from where almost nobody looks onto the first results people read.

## What we changed

The lift came from compounding small, correct decisions rather than one trick. The work split across two fronts.

### Content

We rewrote and restructured pages so each one answered a specific question a buyer actually searches for, with the headings, depth, and internal links that tell Google what the page is about. Thin and overlapping pages were consolidated so the site spoke with one clear voice per topic instead of competing against itself. Better-matched titles and descriptions did the work on click-through rate: the listing finally said what the page delivered.

### Technical

We fixed the foundations that let Google crawl and index the site cleanly: site health and crawlability issues, page structure and metadata, internal linking, and on-page performance. None of it is glamorous, and all of it is the difference between a page Google can rank and one it skips. These were the changes that moved average position the furthest, because they unblocked pages that were already good enough to rank but were being held back by the site around them.

Alongside the content and technical work, we tracked movement against the baseline throughout, so every change could be tied to what it did rather than guessed at.
