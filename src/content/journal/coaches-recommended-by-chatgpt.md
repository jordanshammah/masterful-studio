        <p>When a prospect asks ChatGPT who is the best executive coach for new VPs, or business coach for solopreneurs, the model names coaches it can verify across the surfaces it reads: your site, your reviews, directories, LinkedIn, podcasts, and press. Coaches who structure those surfaces around the exact questions clients ask get named. The rest stay invisible in a channel they cannot see, and most of your competitors have not addressed this at all yet.</p>

        <h2>The shift, in numbers</h2>
        <p>AI search visits grew 42.8% year over year to 27.4 billion in Q1 2026, while Google search grew 2.4%. AI search visitors convert at roughly 4.4 times the value of average organic visitors. Industry coverage in 2026 calls AI visibility for coaches a blue ocean, with early movers capturing a disproportionate share of AI-referred leads. The window where showing up is cheap stays open while your competitors ignore it.</p>

        <h2>How the model decides which coach to name</h2>
        <p>Four signals carry the decision. Relevance: a page on your site answers the exact question the prospect asked. Verification: your name, niche, and claims read the same across every surface the model checks. Readable social proof: reviews with specifics the model can quote. Recency: fresh content and recent mentions. Certifications and follower counts weigh less than a machine-confirmable niche and machine-confirmable results.</p>

        <h2>The 5 surfaces, in priority order</h2>
        <p><strong>1. One page per client question.</strong> Put a number in the first 50 words. "How much does executive coaching cost" beats "transformational coaching for ambitious leaders" on every machine surface that matters.</p>
        <p><strong>2. Reviews with outcomes.</strong> "Maria helped me raise my rates 40% in 4 months" beats "amazing coach". Ask clients for the number and the timeframe.</p>
        <p><strong>3. A LinkedIn article each month, niche in the headline.</strong> LinkedIn articles get cited inside AI answers. Our own day 0 logs caught them inside Google AI Overview panels. The headline should name your niche, because the headline is what the model matches.</p>
        <p><strong>4. Two or three high-fit directories, plus podcasts.</strong> Pick the directories your niche searches and the podcasts your clients hear. Each one is another surface where the model can confirm you exist and confirm your niche.</p>
        <p><strong>5. Person and Organization schema, with consistent naming.</strong> Mark up who you are and what you do, and use the same name and niche wording everywhere. Conflicting data makes the model hedge, and a hedging model names someone else.</p>

        <h2>The 30-day starting plan</h2>
        <p><strong>Week 1.</strong> List 15 questions prospects ask. Run each through ChatGPT and Perplexity and log which coaches get named.</p>
        <p><strong>Week 2.</strong> Publish two answer pages against the weakest answers in your log.</p>
        <p><strong>Week 3.</strong> Request 5 outcome-specific reviews and publish your first LinkedIn article.</p>
        <p><strong>Week 4.</strong> Re-run the 15 questions, compare against week 1, and repeat what moved.</p>
