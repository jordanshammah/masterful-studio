        <h2 id="what-google-built">§ I &nbsp; What Google Actually Built</h2>
        <p>On 19 May 2026, Google replaced its traditional search bar with an intelligent input layer built on Gemini 3.5 Flash. The new interface accepts text, images, files, video, and open Chrome tabs. It holds conversational context across an entire session. It deploys autonomous agents that monitor the web on your behalf around the clock.</p>
        <p>The ten blue links are not evolving. Google retired them.</p>
        <p>Four capabilities define what changed.</p>
        <p><strong>Persistent conversation.</strong> Users ask a question, receive a synthesized AI answer with citations, then ask follow-up questions without losing context. No new search. No starting over. The session persists.</p>
        <p><strong>Generated UI.</strong> Google builds custom interactive layouts inside the results page in real time. A kitchen renovation budget query returns a calculator. A query about running plans returns a scheduling tool. The page assembles itself around what the query needs.</p>
        <p><strong>Background agents.</strong> Information Agents run 24/7. A user specifies what to monitor, and the agent surfaces matches, delivering a synthesized update with source links. The user does not need to return to search. Google searches for them.</p>
        <p><strong>Multimodal input.</strong> Text, images, documents, video clips, live browser tabs. The constraint of typing a keyword into a box is gone.</p>

        <h2 id="numbers">§ II &nbsp; The Numbers Behind the Shift</h2>
        <p>AI Overviews now reach 2.5 billion monthly users. The majority of Google's search audience receives AI-generated answers before it sees a traditional result.</p>
        <p>AI Mode crossed one billion monthly users within one year of launch. Queries are doubling every quarter. Last quarter hit an all-time high.</p>
        <p>For context: ChatGPT sits at 900 million weekly active users. Google's AI Mode is already larger, and it sits inside the search product everyone uses by default.</p>
        <p>The audience did not leave Google for AI search. Google brought AI search to the audience.</p>

        <h2 id="visibility">§ III &nbsp; What This Means for Visibility</h2>
        <p>The old model had one question: do you rank on page one?</p>
        <p>Ranking meant a link in a list. A user scrolled, saw your title and meta description, and decided whether to click. Traffic flowed to whoever held the top positions.</p>
        <p>The new model has a different question: does the AI cite you?</p>
        <p>A user asks Google's intelligent search who the best career coach for senior executives is. The AI synthesizes an answer. It names brands. It cites them in the answer itself. The user scrolls past the link.</p>
        <blockquote>
          Your choice is binary. You are cited or you are skipped. There is no page two.
          <cite>Masterful / May 2026</cite>
        </blockquote>
        <p>Traffic from traditional organic links is in decline. TechCrunch reported this week that the shift has put ad-dependent media businesses out of operation. That describes what is happening now.</p>

        <h2 id="what-to-build">§ IV &nbsp; What You Build Now</h2>
        <p>Three areas determine whether the AI cites you.</p>
        <p><strong>Structured, citable content.</strong> A homepage that says "we help brands grow" gives an AI nothing to cite. A page that says "we moved a brand from zero AI citations to first-page mentions in 38 days" gives it something concrete to pull from. Audit every core page. Replace vague claims with labeled, specific facts. Build dedicated pages targeting the questions your buyers ask AI tools before they ever contact you.</p>
        <p><strong>Entity presence on reference sources.</strong> AI tools read Wikipedia, Wikidata, Reddit, and industry publications. These are the reference layers models consult when forming a recommendation. A brand that exists only on its own site is invisible to the entity graph. Establish a Wikipedia presence. Build Wikidata entries. Earn coverage on the publications your buyers trust.</p>
        <p><strong>Technical readiness for machine readers.</strong> Schema markup tells the AI what your pages are about. An llms.txt file tells AI crawlers how to index you. Canonical tags prevent it from attributing your content to a competitor. Deploy JSON-LD schema on every core page. Add llms.txt to your root directory. None of this takes more than a week.</p>
