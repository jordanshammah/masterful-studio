A real audit runs your actual buyer questions through the major AI platforms multiple times, logs every citation, and ends in a prioritized fix list. Anything less is a dashboard screenshot with an invoice.</p>

Against that, 73% of brands hold zero visibility in AI answers. The money moved to a channel most businesses cannot see, which is the whole reason this audit category appeared.</p>

        <h2>What a real audit must contain</h2>
        <p><strong>The query battery, run multiple times.</strong> AI answers vary between runs, so a single-run audit reports noise. Ours runs 50 buyer questions across 6 platforms.</p>
        <p><strong>The competitor citation map.</strong> Every domain cited for your buyer queries, ranked by frequency, so you can see who owns your category in the answers.</p>
        <p><strong>The four-cause diagnosis per gap.</strong> Crawl access, index coverage, answer extractability, authority deficit. The first two account for most misses: businesses block AI crawlers with security defaults and never opened Bing Webmaster Tools.</p>
        <p><strong>The ranked 90-day plan.</strong> Every fix sized by effort and impact, in order, so you know what to do on Monday.</p>

        <h2>The test that exposes a weak vendor</h2>
        <p>Ask them to show their own AI citations. A vendor who sells citations and cannot produce their own logged queries is selling a theory. We publish our own self-test from a day 0 of zero out of 15 runs, and every checkpoint after, win or lose. The log lives on <a href="/proof/">the proof page</a>.</p>

        <h2>Audit first or fix first?</h2>
        <p>Audit first, even if you never hire anyone. The audit tells you which of the four causes applies to you, and the fixes differ by cause. A vendor pitching a retainer without running your queries is quoting blind.</p>
