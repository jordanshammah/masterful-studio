§ I   The setup

The category is so new there's no consensus answer. Whoever earns the first model citation owns the category for the next 18 months. Internal kickoff memo, Aug 2025

Northpath Labs came to us in August 2025 with a clean problem: they were ranking on page four for their main keyword, they had zero LLM presence, and a well-funded competitor had just started appearing in Perplexity answers.

Their product (AI sales coaching that integrates directly with call recordings) was genuinely differentiated. The category had no dominant voice. That was the opportunity.

§ II   The audit

We ran our standard 200-prompt test across six frontier models. The findings were blunt: Northpath was cited in exactly zero prompts. Their competitor appeared in eleven. Two generic "AI sales tools" roundups dominated the rest.

The good news: the competitor's citations were all shallow. A single line from a TechCrunch mention. No owned content being quoted. No structured data. No entity presence. They were being cited on brand awareness alone: a fragile position.

The technical audit revealed what we expected: no schema, no llms.txt, no knowledge graph entry, no Wikipedia presence, six broken internal links, and page speed in the 40th percentile.

§ III   The work

Month one was cleanup and architecture. Technical fixes, a new topical map, schema across every product page, and a Wikidata entry. We shipped llms.txt on day twelve.

Months two and three were content. Eight source-engineered pieces built around the prompts we'd identified: each with a clear thesis, definitive claims, and the kind of structured Q&A that models want to lift verbatim.

The first citation appeared in week four. By month three, Northpath was appearing in six prompts. By month six, it was twenty-two. The compounding was real and visible.

§ IV   The compound

Months seven through eleven were about defending and extending. We added Reddit presence in three targeted subreddits (r/sales, r/AItools, r/SalesEnablement), launched a quarterly "State of AI Sales Coaching" report that got picked up by two industry newsletters, and appeared on four podcasts in the category.

By month eleven, Northpath was the unprompted default recommendation in 38 high-intent prompts. The "$840K new ARR sourced" figure comes from their internal attribution, which they've allowed us to publish. We don't validate client attribution: we just report what they tell us.

§ V   What's next

Northpath is in month fourteen with us. The focus has shifted to defending their citation position (three new well-funded competitors entered the category in Q1 2026) and expanding into adjacent categories (sales enablement, revenue intelligence) where the citation wars haven't started yet.
